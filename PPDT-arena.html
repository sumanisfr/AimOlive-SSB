Picture Perception & Discussion Test

Stage-I Screening Test Briefing

STANDARD OPERATING PROCEDURE

The PPDT is the second test of the Stage-I screening procedure, conducted immediately after the OIR test. It is designed to assess your raw perception and spontaneous thought process.

  1. Observation (30 Secs): A hazy picture is shown. You must identify the number of characters, their age, gender, mood, and the core action.
  2. Writing (4 Mins): You must write a constructive story around the picture, with a clear past (what led to the situation), present (what is happening), and future (outcome).
  3. Narration & Discussion: Candidates are divided into groups of 15-20. Each narrates their story in under 1 minute. Once completed, the group discusses to arrive at a common consensus story.
OFFICER LIKE QUALITIES (OLQs) ASSESSED
  • Effective Intelligence & Imagination: Ability to perceive hazy stimuli correctly and form a logical, practical story.
  • Self-Confidence: Clarity in narration and firmness in presenting your views during discussion.
  • Power of Expression: Articulating thoughts concisely and clearly under time pressure.
  • Social Adaptability: Willingness to listen to others and accept valid points during the group discussion.
  • Initiative & Influence: Ability to steer the group towards a consensus without being domineering.
